Stochastic Optimal Control of a Sailboat

Authors

  • Cole Miles, Cornell University
  • Alexander Vladimirsky, Cornell University

Abstract

In match race sailing, competitors must steer their ships upwind in the presence of unpredictable evolving weather. This combined with the tacking motions necessary to make upwind progress naturally lend this system to be modeled as a hybrid stochastic optimal control problem. We accelerate a semi-Lagrangian approach introduced by Ferretti and Festa by reformulating the state space in one fewer dimension and designing an adaptive timestep discretization which is very nearly causal. Additionally, we improve the accuracy of evaluating a switching operator by integrating over potential final wind states during a tack. We present a collection of numerical experiments demonstrating our results.
